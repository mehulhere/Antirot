# Antirot iOS Rams Scorecard

1. Good design is innovative — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: The interface applies familiar translucent-card and floating-tab patterns repeatedly rather than improving the interaction model (`01-evidence.md#visual-evidence`).
   Justification: It imitates current mobile glass conventions with minor palette variation.

2. Good design makes a product useful — Score: 2/3
   Evidence: The primary Coach action and communication affordances are available, but Tasks lacks row actions and adjacent surfaces add friction (`01-evidence.md#structural-evidence`).
   Justification: The core task completes, yet unnecessary controls and incomplete adjacent actions add steps.

3. Good design is aesthetic — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: A central palette exists, but equal-weight glass containers, broad spacing/type scales, weak contrast, and screenshot overlap create multiple visible inconsistencies (`01-evidence.md#visual-evidence`).
   Justification: The system has tokens but not a convincing hierarchy.

4. Good design makes a product understandable — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Noninteractive chevrons, a hidden-draft add button, unconditional connection copy, and ambiguous labels create more than three unclear controls (`01-evidence.md#copy-and-honesty-evidence`).
   Justification: Several primary affordances cannot be predicted correctly from their presentation.

5. Good design is unobtrusive — Score: 0/3
   Evidence: Headers, segmented controls, cards, chat, actions, and navigation all use similarly prominent chrome; navigation visibly occludes content (`01-evidence.md#visual-evidence`).
   Justification: Chrome dominates the content instead of receding behind it.

6. Good design is honest — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Zero work can render as 5%, estimated durations appear as Focus, and several chevrons/buttons do not perform their implied behavior (`01-evidence.md#copy-and-honesty-evidence`).
   Justification: Multiple presentation claims do not map one-to-one to available data or behavior.

7. Good design is long-lasting — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Heavy translucent cards, oversized radii, floating navigation, and ornamental surface effects are strong trend markers (`01-evidence.md#visual-evidence`).
   Justification: The design reads as a specific glassmorphism moment rather than a durable editorial system.

8. Good design is thorough down to the last detail — Score: 1/3
   Evidence: Loading/error semantics conflict, focus and disabled appearance are underdefined, muted contrast is weak, and gesture accessibility is incomplete (`01-evidence.md#state-and-accessibility-evidence`).
   Justification: More than two critical states or details remain rough.

9. Good design is environmentally friendly — Score: 2/3
   Evidence: This is a native dark UI with motion gated, but redundant stage assets add roughly 1.49 MB and initial data settling uses sequential requests (`01-evidence.md#weight-and-attention-evidence`).
   Justification: It is comparatively light and motion-aware, with identifiable resource and request waste.

10. Good design is as little design as possible — Score: 0/3
    Evidence: Card stacks, redundant metrics, a decorative quote, faux Stats rows, overlapping chat affordances, and persistent chrome dominate several screens (`01-evidence.md#structural-evidence`).
    Justification: Removing multiple elements would improve rather than break the primary task.

## Total

**10/30**
